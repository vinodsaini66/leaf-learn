Common name: kapok , Java kapok, silk-cotton.
Botanical name: Ceiba pentandra
Places found:  Mexico, Central America and the Caribbean. Also found in Asia.
Medicinal uses: 
1. Seeds produce an oil that is used locally in soap and can be used as fertilizer.
2. It is used as a diuretic, aphrodisiac, and to treat headache, as well as type II diabetes.
